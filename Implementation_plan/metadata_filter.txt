Flow 1: Automatic Extraction from the JD (AI-Driven)
If the recruiter just uploads a raw Job Description (e.g., "We are looking for a Senior Data Scientist with at least 5 years of experience..."):

Our JD Rewriting Agent (app/agents/jd_rewrite_agent.py) parses the raw JD text.
The agent uses the LLM to extract these structured requirements:
role: "Data Scientist"
min_experience: 5
domain: "Data Science"
The system then automatically applies the metadata.total_experience_years >= 5 filter to Qdrant behind the scenes, ensuring the AI only ranks candidates who meet the baseline experience required by the JD.
Flow 2: Explicit Dashboard Filters (User-Driven)
On the recruiter's frontend dashboard, we can display sidebar filters:

A slider for Minimum Experience (0 to 15+ years).
A dropdown for Domain selection. If a recruiter changes the slider to 5 years and clicks search, the frontend sends min_experience=5 to the backend API, which appends it as a filter condition to your Qdrant query.
Summary:
Using Strategy B lets you do both:

The vector search will rank candidates based on how well their skills match the JD description.
The metadata filters (whether extracted by the LLM from the JD, or selected manually by the recruiter) will strictly filter the results.
